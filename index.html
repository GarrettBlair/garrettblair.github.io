Garrett J. Blair, PhD

Post-doctoral researcher

Fenton Lab, New York University

Neuroscientist studying spatial memory

I am currently at NYU working in André Fenton's lab, combining calcium imaging and electrophyiological methods to evaluate memory dynamics across various brain regions. I'm primarily interested in how the hippocampus coordinates itself with cortical brain regions during memory retrieval and spatial navigation.

After beginning my undergraduate research at UC Irvine in the Chubb-Wright lab, I recieved my PhD from the Deptartment of Psychology at UCLA in 2021, where I studied hippocampal place cells in Tad Blair's lab (no relation, but he is great).

I primarily focused on applying calcium imaging to the rat so that we could evaluate long term memory dynamics within the same cell population. I've collaborated with the Miniscope corsortium at UCLA to expand upon this project (e.g. the MiniLFOV 1, 3) and help other labs adapt the technique to suite their research needs (in collaboration with the Izquierdo lab 2 and Adhikari lab 4).
